##  Problem Statement  

Modern businesses rely on **complex and interconnected supply chains** involving multiple suppliers, regions, and logistics networks. While this interconnectedness improves efficiency, it also increases vulnerability to disruptions.  

External factors such as **extreme weather conditions, transportation delays, and supplier failures** can interrupt the smooth flow of goods. Even a single point of failure in the chain can create cascading effects across the entire business operation.  

---

##  Major Causes of Supply Chain Disruptions  

###  Environmental Factors  
- Heavy rainfall and floods  
- Extreme heatwaves  
- Poor air quality conditions  

These conditions can delay transportation, reduce workforce efficiency, or completely halt logistics operations.  

###  Operational and Logistics Issues  
- Traffic congestion  
- Route blockages  
- Vehicle or infrastructure failures  

These issues lead to delayed deliveries and inefficient supply movement.  

###  Supplier-Level Failures  
- Delayed production cycles  
- Inventory shortages at supplier end  
- Unreliable or inconsistent suppliers  

These failures directly impact downstream businesses and disrupt inventory planning.  

---

##  The Core Issue  

Most businesses today operate with **limited visibility into future risks**. Existing systems are largely reactive, meaning actions are taken only after a disruption has already occurred.  

### Key Gaps  
- Lack of early warning systems  
- No real-time risk assessment  
- Inability to estimate financial and operational impact  

### Impact  
- Frequent stock shortages  
- Revenue losses due to unmet demand  
- Decline in customer satisfaction and trust  

---

##  Target Persona / Stakeholders  

###  The Supply-Dependent Business  

REX focuses on businesses that rely heavily on **consistent and uninterrupted supply flow**, including:  

- Retail businesses managing physical inventory  
- E-commerce platforms handling high order volumes  
- Small and medium enterprises with limited buffer capacity  
- Inventory-driven operations dependent on just-in-time supply  

### ⚙️ Operational Context  
These businesses operate with multiple suppliers across regions and depend on timely deliveries and accurate inventory planning. Even minor disruptions can affect their ability to fulfill orders.  

###  Needs  
- Early visibility into potential supplier risks  
- Reliable system to monitor supply chain health  
- Ability to estimate business impact before disruption  
- Guidance on alternative actions to reduce losses  

###  Pain Points  
- No prior warning before supplier failure  
- Difficulty identifying weak links in the supply chain  
- Sudden inventory shortages  
- Financial losses due to delayed or missed deliveries  
- Dependence on manual decision-making during crises  

---

##  Problem Scope  

###  Scope Includes  
- Monitoring supplier reliability and external risk factors  
- Predicting potential disruptions before they occur  
- Estimating business impact (inventory and revenue loss)  
- Providing actionable recommendations  

###  Boundaries and Requirements  
- Designed as a **decision-support system**, not a full logistics platform  
- Focus on **prediction and impact analysis**, not route optimization  
- Requires basic supplier data (location, reliability, supply volume)  
- Supports **simulated or limited real-time data**  

###  Limitations  
- No deep ERP integration  
- Uses simplified prediction models initially  
- Focused on small to medium-scale supply chains  

 Goal: Demonstrate how **predictive intelligence improves resilience**, not replace existing systems

##  Our Solution  

###  AI-Powered Supply Chain Resilience Platform  

**REX: Resilient Everflow AI** is an intelligent system that ensures continuous supply flow by predicting disruptions and enabling proactive decisions.  

---

###  Solution Overview  

The platform continuously monitors supplier data along with external risk factors such as weather and logistics conditions.  

It evaluates disruption probability, assigns risk levels, estimates downstream impact, and provides actionable recommendations to minimize losses.  

---

###  Core Components  

- **Supplier Monitoring Layer**  
  Tracks supplier performance and external influencing factors  

- **Risk Prediction Engine**  
  Identifies potential disruptions and classifies risk levels  

- **Impact Analysis Module**  
  Estimates inventory gaps and revenue loss  

- **Recommendation Engine**  
  Suggests mitigation strategies like switching suppliers or increasing stock  

---

###  Key Features & Innovation  

- **Real-Time Risk Monitoring**  
  Continuous tracking of supplier and environmental conditions  

- **Disruption Prediction Engine**  
  Categorizes suppliers into safe, medium risk, and high risk  

- **Impact Analysis System**  
  Provides clear estimation of business loss  

- **Smart Recommendation Engine**  
  Suggests practical preventive actions  

 REX transforms supply chains from **reactive → proactive systems** using predictive intelligence

##  System Workflow  

![Everflow AI Workflow](Docs/Everflow_workflow.png)

###  Process Flow  

1. Business registers and adds supplier details  
2. System collects data from weather APIs, manual inputs, and simulated logistics data  
3. Risk evaluation is performed using rule-based logic  
4. If thresholds are crossed, disruption risk is flagged  
5. Suppliers are classified into risk categories  
6. Impact analysis estimates potential loss  
7. Recommendations are generated  
8. Results are displayed on the dashboard
